Species Index
Kelp Lace Bryozoan
Membranacea serrilamella
Typical size: 1-3” diameter
ID: Series of very small thin white connected rectangles that form a colony. 
Comments:  This white bryozoan grows on kelp.  I note kelp bryozoan most often in fall months when the broadleaf kelp is at its peak.
Orange Encrusting Bryozoan
Schizoporella unicornis
Typical size: Variable
ID: Thin, orange crust with small pores.
Comments:  I typically note this rather unspectacular bryozoan on rocks at depths of les than 50 feet.
Sculptured Bryozoan
Hippodiplosia insculpti
Typical size: 2-8” diameter
ID: Series of intermingled ear-like folds.
Comments:  The sculpted bryozaon is an artistic spectacle.  I often note comparitively small specimens in Puget Sound and the San Juan Islands attached to rocky reefs. Robust specimens, like the one pictured, are readily found throughout the Cape Flattery area.  Note the blue tinge to the water.  Ahh, Cape Flattery....
Southern Staghorn Bryozoan
Heteropora pacifica
Typical size: 0.25-0.50” height
ID: Low growing, sprawling bryozoan.  Uneven, rounded lobes yellow and brown in color.
Comments:  This bryozoan dominates many rocky areas in northern British Columbia and can give the rocky substrate a coral reef-like appearance.  Photographed at Solander Island off Brook's Peninsula.
White Branching Bryozoan
Diaperoecia californica
Typical size: 2-6” diameter
ID: Hard texture. White and yellow branches that grow relatively flat. 
Comments:  The first time I noted this bryozoan was in the San Juans Islands.  With it's white color and flat pattern, I thought it was a dying staghorn bryozoan.  Had coral bleaching reached the Northwest?  Not at all - it turns out the white branching bryozoan is fairly common throughout the San Juan Islands.
Spriral Bryozoan
Bugula californica
Typical size: 1-1.5” height
ID: Cone-shaped appearance with circular rows of branches.
Comments:  I don't often note this small and bland colored bryozoan, most likely because it usually lives amongst dense invertebrate colonies which often dwarf  it.  I most often find small colonies of this bryozoan clinging to the underside of rocky current swept ledges in the San Juan Islands.
Leaf Crust Bryozoan
Dendrobenia lichenoides
Typical size: 0.5-1” diameter (per leaf)
ID: Tiny, low profile lichen-like encrusttion with fine lattice work.  Irregular shapes. 
Comments:  This is a very inconspicuous bryonzoan that you really have to look for as it is small and tends to blend in with its sourroundings.  Also called a sea-lichen bryozoan.  Photo taken at KVI Tower Reef in Puget Sound.
Fan Bryozoan
Dendrobeania murrayana
Typical size: 3-5” diameter
ID: Series of flat branches emanating from a common source.    Lies relatively flat. 
Comments:  I have only noted this bryozoan when diving the Canadian Gulf Islands.  Photographed at Boiling Reef near Saturna Island.
Rusty Bryozoan
Costazia ventricasa
Typical size: 4" across
ID: Orange-brown coloration.  Short, thick branching arms.
Comments:  This unassuming bryozoan dominates section around northern Vancouver Island.  Photgraphed in Lucan Channel in Browning Pass.
Northern Staghorn Bryozoan
Heteropora pacifica
Typical size: 2-4” diameter
ID: Dense yellow antler-like branches, hard to the touch.
Comments:  I often note small staghron bryozoans in Puget Sound and Hood Canal.  I can usually find bigger specimens in the San Juans, and spectacular specimes  in the Cape Flattery area.  Most things do grow bigger on the coast.  The photograph of this staghorn bryozoan was taken at Tatoosh Canyon.
Stick Bryozoan
Microporina borealis
Typical size: 6” across
ID: Series or thin, branching arms.  Each arm consists of  segmented stick-like segments.
Comments: Another of the common bryozoan off Vancouver island.  Photgraphed at Lucan Channel in Browning Pass, but could have been photgraphed at most sites around norther Vancouver Island.
Delicate Staghorn Bryozoan
heteropora alaskensis
Typical size: 1-3” diameter
ID: Narrow branching arms, much thinner than staghron bryozoan.  Usualy white/tan in color and tucked into areas somewhat out of the current.
Comments: The unassuming bryozoan does very well in parts of the San Juan Islands.   As it tends to be overshadowing by more striking species, it is often overlooked.  Thousands of specimens can be found where this photo was taken - Davidson Rocks in the San Juan Islands.
Smooth Leather Bryozoan
Alcyanidium pedunculatum
Typical size: 2-6" height
ID: Short spikes protruding from fleshy extensions.  Typically yellow to tan in Washington waters.
Comments:  Another of the commonly encountered bryozoans in the San Juan Islands.  This seceimen was photographed at Davidson Rocks off the south end of Lopez Island.  Like all bryozoans, this one is a colonial animal.  
Lattice-Work Bryozoan
Phidolopora pacifica
Typical size: 2-4” diameter
ID: Unique and intricate folds exhibiting a delicate lattice-work. 
Comments: I don;t often note this bryozoan - and when I do, it is relatively small (1-2 inches).  This specimen, photographed at Ruth Rocks in Browning Pass, British Columbia, is about 3" across.  With larger anmials, the center may be dead as the new growth continues outward.
White Tuft Bryozoan
Crisia spp
Typical size: 2-4” height
ID: Heavily branched - brown to white color.  Often found inverted or growing amongst other invertebrates.
Comments:  There are many species of white tuft bryozoans that are hard to distinguish from one another without examination in a lab.  Photograph taken at Northwest Passage in Browning Pass, British Columbia.
